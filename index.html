I am a graduate student in Computer Science at the University of Chicago. I spend most of my time on Deep Learning and High-Performance Computing problems.

I've spent some time as a RA and Researcher at the UChicago Booth Center for Applied Artificial Intelligence. My research was on uncovering inductive biases and their causes and effects in popular deep learning models such as AlexNet, ResNet and DenseNet.

Research Interests

I am most interested in the science of learning; how neural activities are computed, cached and propogated through the brain effciently. Toward understanding this, my work explores Deep Learning and High-Performance Computing problems. More specifically, generative frameworks (GAN, VAE, DDMP), energy-based models (EBMs) and self-supervised learning (SSL). Most of my work has been in the Computer Vision domain, however, I have dipped my toes in NLP and time-series (I wonder which classification has more papers on autoregressive models). Feel free to explore my publications and open source projects for more details.

Though my interest in Deep Learning is out of curiosity, my work in HPC is out of necessity. I have always been excited about a computer's architecture and HPC enables me to explore the limits of computation on large scale problems. This has led me to develop several open source simulation tools.

Broader Interests

Though I am most interested in the science of learning, I often take an engineering approach to understanding it. This has led me to develop several open source tools and libraries that can be found here.

I have thoroughly enjoyed exploring Reinforcement Learning, Game Theory, Computational Learning Theory, and Neuroscience, as a means of inspiration and curiosity. My brief foray into Boosting and Boostability drives me to create more probabilistic models for efficient learning i.e. "focus on the hard cases".

What excites me about Deep Learning is its infancy and novelty. What drives me is the potential to impact and an unquenched curiosity.
